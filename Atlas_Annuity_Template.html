The Basics

What Is an Annuity?

In Plain English
An annuity is a contract between you and an insurance company. You contribute money — either all at once or over time — and the insurance company promises to protect your principal, grow your money, and pay you a guaranteed income stream you can never outlive.
Three Core Jobs
  • ✦Protect your money from market losses
  • ✦Grow it tax-deferred over time
  • ✦Turn it into income you can never outlive
Types of Annuities
MYGA — Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity
Locked-in guaranteed rate for 3–10 years. The CD alternative with higher yields and tax deferral. No market exposure whatsoever.
FIA — Fixed Index Annuity
Interest linked to a market index like the S&P 500 — with a 0% floor. You participate in gains and are completely shielded from losses.
SPIA / DIA — Income Annuities
Convert a lump sum into guaranteed lifetime income. Simple, predictable, and impossible to outlive — the pension you build yourself.
Annuity products involve risk and are subject to insurance company claims-paying ability.

Setting the Record Straight

Annuity Myths vs. Reality

✕  Myth
"You lose control of your money."
✓  Reality
Most annuities allow you to withdraw up to 10% of your account value per year penalty-free. You always remain the owner of the contract.
✕  Myth
"Annuities are loaded with hidden fees."
✓  Reality
Many modern annuities — especially MYGAs and base FIAs — have zero annual fees. Optional riders carry a disclosed, transparent fee. Everything is in writing before you sign.
✕  Myth
"You can't access your money."
✓  Reality
Annuities have a surrender period — just like a CD. Free withdrawal provisions, nursing home waivers, and terminal illness riders give you meaningful access when life happens.
✕  Myth
"They're too complicated to understand."
✓  Reality
The core concept is simple: put money in, let it grow protected, take income out. Our job is to make the details clear — because it should be simple.
Product features vary by carrier and state. Free withdrawal provisions, riders, and surrender schedules are defined in the contract. Review all terms before purchasing.

Safe Money Alternative

MYGA — The Smarter CD

What Is a MYGA?
A Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity works exactly like a bank CD — but issued by an insurance company. You lock in a guaranteed interest rate for a set term (3, 5, 7, or 10 years), your principal is 100% protected, and your money grows tax-deferred until you need it.
Who Is It Perfect For?
  • ✦CD rollovers — your CD just matured
  • ✦Cash sitting idle in a savings account
  • ✦Money market funds earning low yields
  • ✦Conservative investors wanting safety + growth
  • ✦Pre-retirees building a safe money bucket
🔒
Guaranteed Rate — Locked In
Your rate is set at the start and doesn't change. No surprises, no resets, no market risk. What the contract says is what you get.
📈
Tax-Deferred Growth
Unlike a bank CD, you don't pay taxes each year on interest earned. Your money compounds without the annual tax drag — keeping more working for you.
💧
10% Annual Free Withdrawal
Most MYGAs allow you to withdraw up to 10% of your account value each year without any surrender charge — so you're not fully locked in.
🏦
State Guaranty Association Backing
Annuities are backed by state guaranty associations (similar to FDIC for banks), providing an additional layer of protection for your principal.
MYGA rates and terms vary by carrier and state. State guaranty association coverage limits vary; check your state's limits at nolhga.com.

The Growth Engine

What Is a Fixed Index Annuity?

FIA in Plain English
A Fixed Index Annuity is an insurance contract that links your interest credits to the performance of a market index — like the S&P 500 — without you owning any stocks. You participate in market gains up to a cap, and a 0% floor means you can never lose principal or previously credited interest due to market declines.
Think of It This Way
Imagine you could invest in the stock market — but with a safety net that guarantees you never go below zero. That's an FIA. When the market goes up, you earn (up to a cap). When it goes down, you simply earn nothing. Your principal is always safe.
0% Floor
The single most powerful feature. When markets fall, your account value does not. You never lose a dollar of principal or previously credited gains due to index declines.
Index-Linked
Credits are tied to real market indices — S&P 500, Nasdaq, DJIA, and others. You don't own stocks, but your growth potential moves with them.
Tax-Deferred
Growth inside an FIA compounds without annual taxation — giving your money a compounding advantage over taxable accounts.
FIA interest is not directly invested in the market. Caps, participation rates, and spreads vary by carrier and product. Past index performance does not guarantee future interest credits.

Step by Step

How Annual Point-to-Point Crediting Works

1
Contract Anniversary
(Start Point)
Your contract anniversary date is recorded. The index value on that day becomes your starting point for the year.
›
2
Index Tracks for 12 Months
Over the next year, the chosen index moves up or down. You don't own stocks — you're watching the scoreboard from the sidelines.
›
3
Next Anniversary
(End Point)
On your next anniversary, the index is measured again. The % change from start to end is calculated.
›
4
Floor & Cap
Applied
Positive? Capped at the contract cap (e.g. 10%). Zero or negative? The 0% floor applies. You never lose principal.
›
5
Interest
Credited
The calculated interest is credited to your account value — permanently locked in. The cycle resets for next year.
✦   Key Takeaway: With Annual Point-to-Point, you participate in market GAINS up to a cap, and are completely shielded from market LOSSES.

Never Outlive Your Money

The Income Rider — Your Personal Pension

1
Accumulation Phase
Your premium grows via index-linked credits. An income rider adds a separate "benefit base" that may grow at a guaranteed rollup rate (e.g. 6–8% per year) — regardless of index performance.
2
Income Activation
When you're ready — typically after a 5–10 year deferral — you "turn on" the income. Your payout is determined by your benefit base and your age at activation. The longer you wait, the higher your payout.
3
Guaranteed for Life
Once income begins, you receive a check every month — for life. Even if your account value drops to zero, the insurance company continues paying. Joint-life options available.
4
Remaining Value to Heirs
If you pass away before exhausting the account value, the remaining balance passes to your beneficiaries — so nothing is "lost."

Advanced Tax Strategy

PIRC — Piecemeal Internal Roth Conversion

What Is a PIRC?
A Piecemeal Internal Roth Conversion is a strategy available inside select qualified IRA annuities that lets you convert portions of your tax-deferred value into Roth status — in controlled amounts — over multiple years. Instead of one large taxable event, you convert strategically, staying within your optimal tax bracket each year.
Why It Matters
  • ✦Spread the tax burden over years — not one large bill
  • ✦Convert in low-income years before RMDs begin
  • ✦Reduce future Required Minimum Distributions
  • ✦Build a tax-free income stream for retirement
  • ✦Leave tax-free assets to heirs — not a tax liability
How It Works
1
Select the Right Annuity Upfront
Not all contracts permit internal Roth conversions. Support for PIRC must be available in the chosen annuity at the time of application — it cannot be added to an existing contract.
2
Accumulate Tax-Deferred
Your IRA annuity grows protected by the FIA's 0% floor, building the pool available for future conversions.
3
Convert Annually — Work With Your CPA
Each year, work with your CPA or tax professional to determine the ideal conversion amount — typically sized to fill your bracket without pushing into the next. You pay tax now; it becomes Roth going forward.
4
Tax-Free Growth & Income
Converted funds grow tax-free and can be accessed tax-free in retirement — annuity protection meets Roth advantage.
⚠ Critical — Product Selection Matters
Support for PIRC must be available in the chosen annuity at the time of application. Ask us which carriers support this strategy before you apply.
✦   PIRC turns your IRA annuity into a two-phase tax engine: tax-deferred growth today, converted to tax-free income tomorrow — at a pace you and your CPA control.
PIRC strategies involve tax implications subject to IRS rules governing Roth conversions (IRC §408A). Consult your CPA or qualified tax professional regarding conversion amounts and timing. This is not tax or legal advice. Not all annuity products support internal Roth conversions — product selection must occur prior to application.
